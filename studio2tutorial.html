Module 2: Using Studio 2.0 for Planning

Studio is a CAD (Computer Aided Design) tool specifically made to help builders plan a certain creation, like a MOC, before ordering parts and building it in real life. For FLL, it helps students share their ideas without having to build something in real life first. Just imagine if you had to take apart your entire tool just to make a simple change! That would be strenuous, and doesn't yield results unless you are very experienced. In short, Studio is a designing tool optimized for efficiency.

Download Studio 2.0

If you haven't already, download Studio for your device using this link below to the direct Bricklink site.

Download Studio 2.0

Simply follow Bricklink's download button, and you should have Studio downloading. Once downloaded, click on the "Downloads" button at the top right of your browser and click on the most recent download to open the package. Unfortunately, Studio doesn't work on certain devices, and a desktop is highly recommended.

Click on "Create New". For now, don't worry about creating an account for Studio, as we'll save anything that comes after this in a folder on Google Drive.

Your Studio Workspace

You will see a screen with a platform to place bricks on, various menus, and some tools at the top left of the window.

This is going to be your Studio Workspace, where you'll be able to build your idea and work on the planning section of the Engineering Design Process. Thankfully, there are some tools within the workspace that will help you do just that.

Important Settings

Before we learn about adding bricks, ensure:

  • You have selected "INSERT". This enables you to add parts.
  • Your window for Studio is in full screen mode.
  • If you are building with SPIKE Prime specifically (for FLL) you can switch between the "45678-1 SPIKE Prime Set" option and "Main".

If you cannot do the last bullet point, it means you do not have the SPIKE Prime set installed onto Studio. In order to access special parts, specifically from certain sets, you need to import them.
